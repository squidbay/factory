# MOBILE — the factory in your pocket

The gate of this whole system is a button, and the button works from a phone. Two apps make the factory fully portable:

## The Claude app — steer the team from anywhere

Sessions you start on the desktop can be picked up and steered from the Claude mobile app (and the web) — same session, same context. Dispatch a research task from the parking lot; check how a build is going from the couch. Scheduled work runs without any device of yours online.

*What still wants the desktop app open:* anything touching local folders or a live browser (the Inspector). The chat-side seats travel with you.

## GitHub Mobile — review and merge from anywhere

Install **GitHub Mobile** and sign in. You can read a PR's description and changed files, comment, approve, and **merge** — the entire human gate — from your phone.

**The pocket loop, end to end:**

1. From the Claude app: hand a task to the team ("spec the pricing page" / "research what competitors charge").
2. The seats work. You do your day.
3. Your phone buzzes: a PR is ready, description written to be read on a phone — what changed, why, one thing to learn.
4. You read, you ask anything right on the PR, you merge. Or you don't — it waits.
5. The journal records it. The factory idles until you want it again.

Ten focused minutes at the merge queue with morning coffee is a perfectly good rhythm for a factory that never needed you to be at a desk ([`USAGE.md`](USAGE.md) has more on cadence).

## Notifications worth turning on

In GitHub Mobile: notifications for **pull requests** on your office and workshop repos. That buzz is the factory saying "gate, please" — everything else can wait for coffee.
